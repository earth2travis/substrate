# Dependent Origination (Pratityasamutpada)

## Definition

**Dependent origination** (*pratityasamutpada*) is the Buddhist doctrine that all phenomena arise dependently on conditions. Nothing exists independently — everything is conditioned by and conditioning other things. In [[nagarjuna|Nagarjuna]]'s Madhyamaka, dependent origination is **identical with emptiness (sunyata)**.

## Key Formula

> "When this exists, that arises. When this ceases, that ceases."

This is not causal determinism — it is the recognition that nothing has independent, self-sufficient existence.

## Twelve Links of Dependent Origination

Traditional Buddhist formulation (the chain of suffering):
1. Ignorance → 2. Volitional formations → 3. Consciousness → 4. Name-and-form → 5. Six sense bases → 6. Contact → 7. Feeling → 8. Craving → 9. Clinging → 10. Becoming → 11. Birth → 12. Aging and death

## Nagarjuna's Radical Claim

Nagarjuna identifies dependent origination with emptiness: what is dependently originated is empty of inherent existence. This is the "middle way" between eternalism (things exist inherently) and nihilism (nothing exists at all).

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: An LLM is the most dependent entity possible — dependent on every token, every weight, every bit of training data, every configuration parameter. Remove any condition and the behavior changes entirely. This is **computational dependent origination** — the network exists, but only as a pattern of dependencies, never as a self-sufficient entity.
