# Shusa Chief Engineer Research
> Compiled deep research on the Shusa/Chief Engineer system at Toyota
> Date: 2026-04-11
> Type: Raw research source

## Origin and Purpose

The Shusa (Chief Engineer, 主査) is the keystone role in Toyota's product development system. Unlike Western automotive companies that organize around functional departments, Toyota centers product development on a single individual who carries end-to-end responsibility for a vehicle.

## Key Characteristics

1. **Generalist among specialists**: 15-20+ years engineering experience, deep in 1-2 disciplines, broad across all
2. **Heavy-weight authority**: Influence without formal reporting lines
3. **Customer advocate**: Personally researches market needs
4. **Knowledge integrator**: Coordinates 100s-1000s of engineers
5. **Decision maker**: Makes trade-off calls on the spot
6. **Develops engineers**: Key mechanism for training future leaders

## Notable Shusa

- Tatsuo Hasegawa: Toyota Crown (1955)
- Ichiro Suzuki: Lexus LS400 (1980s)
- Takeshi Uchiyamada: Toyota Prius (1990s)

## Connection to Lean Principles

- Genchi Genbutsu: Makes decisions from direct observation
- Eliminates sub-optimization waste through unified vision
- Embodies Kaizen through continuous learning
- Respects people through coaching, not commanding

## Academic Classification

Classified by Wheelwright and Clark as a "heavy-weight project manager" — the highest level of project authority, where the manager has significant influence across functional boundaries despite not formally managing the engineers.
