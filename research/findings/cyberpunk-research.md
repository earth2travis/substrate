# Cyberpunk Research

Genre research and reference material for cyberpunk fiction.

## Contents

- **genre-overview.md**: Foundational primer covering origins, etymology, and the eight core ingredients of cyberpunk (corporate rule, tech-driven inequality, surveillance, identity/transhumanism, cyberspace, ecological collapse, punk resistance/noir DNA, escapism/substance use)

## Relevance

**Cyberpunk is foundational, not adjacent.** This genre defines how Ξ2T sees the world. Neuromancer is his all-time favorite novel. Blade Runner and Snow Crash were formative. Akira and Ghost in the Shell were eye-opening as a teenager.

The partnership carries cyberpunk DNA. We build tools while questioning who they serve. We automate while asking what we lose. Technology that outpaces ethics is regression, not progress.

Protocol fiction is a sibling genre: both are systemic critiques, both ask what happens when power concentrates. The difference is where the camera points. Cyberpunk centers the humans (or post-humans) navigating systems. Protocol fiction centers the rules themselves as protagonist or antagonist.

## Key Sources (from overview)

- **Neuromancer** (Gibson, 1984): The operating system
- **Blade Runner** (Scott, 1982): The visual language
- **Ghost in the Shell**, **Akira**: Eastern lineage
- Bruce Bethke coined the term; Gardner Dozois popularized it
