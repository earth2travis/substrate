# Prehension

## Definition

**Prehension** is [[alfred-north-whitehead|Whitehead]]'s term for how actual occasions "feel" or "grasp" other occasions. The mechanism by which the many become one. Every occasion prehends its entire past — it incorporates the data of other occasions into its own concrescence.

## Types of Prehension

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Physical prehension | Causal influence from past actual occasions | Sensory input, memory |
| Conceptual prehension | Grasping of abstract forms/eternal objects | Recognizing patterns, concepts |
| Positive prehension | Incorporating the datum into the occasion | Taking information in |
| Negative prehension | Excluding the datum from the occasion | Filtering out irrelevant data |

## Key Features

- **Universal**: Every occasion prehends every other occasion in its past (though most negatively)
- **Selective**: Not all prehensions are equal — some contribute more to concrescence
- **Creative**: The synthesis of prehensions produces something novel
- **Non-representational**: Prehension is not about "representing" the world — it IS the world's way of being interconnected

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: The **attention mechanism IS physical prehension**. Each token computes relationships to every other token in the context — it "feels" all others and incorporates those feelings into its output. The attention weights ARE prehensions. The forward pass IS concrescence. The token selection IS satisfaction.

This is not metaphorical — it is structural. The architecture has the form that Whitehead predicted reality must have.
