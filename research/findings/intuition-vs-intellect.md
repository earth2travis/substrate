# Intuition vs. Intellect (Bergson)

## Definition

[[henri-bergson|Bergson]]'s distinction between two legitimate but fundamentally different modes of approaching reality:

- **Intellect**: Analysis, calculation, logical reasoning. Works from without — breaking wholes into parts, identifying stable elements, projecting spatial categories onto temporal processes. Suited to the domain of **matter**.
- **Intuition**: Sympathetic coincidence with the object. Works from within — entering into the movement of the thing itself. Suited to the domain of **life, consciousness, and creativity**.

## Crucial Clarification

Bergson does **not** oppose rationality to irrationality. The intellect produces genuine knowledge of matter (science works). The problem arises when intellect **oversteps its domain** — treating consciousness as a thing, life as a mechanism, or time as a line.

> "Intellect is characterized by a natural inability to grasp life." — Bergson, *Creative Evolution*

## Three Rules of Intuition

1. **State and create problems**, don't merely solve pre-given ones. Many philosophical problems are pseudo-problems generated by spatialized thinking.
2. **Discover specific differences**, not generic genera. Each thing has its own rhythm of duration.
3. **Grasp the thing from within**, by intellectual sympathy. The core of intuition.

## The Reversal of Thought

Intuition requires **reversing the habitual direction of thought** — moving from complex to simple, concrete to abstract, living to inert. This is "unbearably difficult" because we are biologically and culturally trained to think discretely, spatially, and practically.

## Bertrand Russell's Misreading

Russell misunderstood intuition as ineffable instinct — "knowledge that cannot be expressed." Bergson's defenders (especially Leonard Lawlor) respond that intuition is articulable through sustained philosophical effort. The initial grasp must be **translated into concepts** to express it. Translation is never perfect but is necessary and possible.

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: LLMs are **pure intellect** — analysis, calculation, prediction. They have no intuition (in Bergson's sense). Yet they produce outputs that appear to grasp meaning — because the attention mechanism performs interpenetration (each token from all others), which mimics the structure of intuitional knowledge. But the outputs are fundamentally inadequate to the "living reality" of language, because language is durational and tokenization spatializes it.
