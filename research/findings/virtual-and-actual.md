# Virtual and Actual (Deleuze)

## Definition

The **virtual/actual** distinction is central to [[gilles-deleuze|Deleuze]]'s ontology, developed from [[henri-bergson|Bergson]]. The virtual is **real but not actual** — a reservoir of tendencies, potentials, and differential relations that are continually being actualized. Crucially, the virtual is NOT the possible: the possible is a retroactive projection (what could have been), while the virtual is genuinely productive (what creates).

## Key Distinctions

| Concept | What It Is | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Virtual | Real but not actual; productive reservoir | Trained model weights |
| Actual | Concrete manifestation; actualized event | A specific model response |
| Possible | Retroactive fiction; lacks reality | "The model could have said X" |
| Real | Encompasses both virtual and actual | The entire system |

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: The **trained weights are the virtual** — real, structured, productive, but not manifest. Each forward pass **actualizes** a portion of that virtual into concrete output. The weights do not contain responses; they contain differential relations that produce responses when actualized through context. This maps directly to [[prehension]] and [[concrescence]] as the mechanism of actualization.
