# Microsolidarity: Five Scales

Source: https://www.microsolidarity.cc/essays/five-scales-of-microsolidarity
Shared by: know
Context: "if you're interested in the social physics of bands, maybe give this webpage a quick read. in their language game, they use the word 'crew'"
Relevance: [[Ξ2T]]'s "Bands Not Corps" principle for next-gen DAOs

## Five Scales

1. **Self as group**: Individual as a collection of parts, networked identity
2. **Dyad** (2 people): Partnership vs. domination spectrum. Foundational relational unit.
3. **Crew** (3-6 people): Small enough for shared mental context, no governance overhead needed. Fits around a dinner table. The primary unit of action. "Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful, committed citizens can change the world." High coordination with minimal explicit rules.
4. **Congregation** (15-150 people): Supports crews to form. Dunbar's number as upper bound. Requires more governance infrastructure. Minimum viable purpose: a dating pool for crewmates to find each other.
5. **Network of congregations**: Multiple congregations in relationship.

## Key Concepts

- **Social physics of belonging**: Developing literacy around how group dynamics change with scale
- **Crew as primary unit**: Most activity happens at the 3-6 person scale. Small enough for implicit coordination, large enough for real impact.
- **Coordination costs scale nonlinearly**: 5 people = 10 relationships; 50 people = 1,225 relationships
- **Partnership vs. domination**: Spectrum that applies at every scale, from self to society
- **Reciprocal co-development**: Crews and congregations develop each other (Loomio/Enspiral example)
- **Crews don't need governance, congregations do**: The transition between these scales is where structure becomes necessary

## Connection to "Bands Not Corps"

Know identified the "crew" concept as mapping to [[Ξ2T]]'s "bands" framing. The parallel:

- **Band = Crew**: 3-6 people, high trust, shared context, minimal overhead
- **Not Corps**: Rejecting the domination/hierarchy model for the partnership model
- Microsolidarity provides academic/practitioner backing for the intuition that small, tight units outperform large hierarchical ones for creative and coordination work
- The "congregation" scale maps to DAO-sized communities that support band formation
- In HIGHER context: **creative crews** (not just "crews" or "bands") as the unit of action
