# Insights: Heijunka for Agent Orchestration

**Source:** `research/management/heijunka-level-scheduling.md`
**Extracted:** 2026-04-12

---

## Insight 1: The "Batching" Trap in AI Work
Most humans and agents work in "batches." We research for days, then code for days, then write for days. This creates **Mura** (unevenness). When we switch contexts, we suffer a "setup time" penalty. Heijunka suggests that **interleaving** tasks (Strategy, Code, Strategy, Code) actually reduces the total cognitive load by keeping all "lines" of thought warm and active.

## Insight 2: The "Heijunka Box" as a Project Board
We can use GitHub Projects or a simple Markdown file in `brain-two` as our **Heijunka Box**. 
*   **Rows:** Sivart (Strategy), Koda (Execution), Ξ2T (Review).
*   **Columns:** Daily intervals.
*   **Cards:** Small, leveled tasks. Instead of one "Build Loom" card, we have 20 small "Loom Component" cards distributed over a month.

## Insight 3: Takt Time for Agents
**Takt Time** is the rate at which we must produce to meet customer demand. For the Agent Factory, our "customer" is Ξ2T. What is our Takt Time? How many "Strategic Insights" or "Shipped Features" do we need per week to keep the factory moving without burnout? Defining this prevents **Muri** (overburden).

## Insight 4: Leveling the "Shared Brain"
If Sivart dumps 50 pages of research into `brain-two` all at once, it creates a bottleneck for Koda. Heijunka dictates that we **level our inputs**. Sivart should provide one "Strategic Spec" per day. Koda should provide one "Code Spec" per day. This keeps the shared nervous system flowing smoothly.

---

## Conclusion
Heijunka is the antidote to the "feast or famine" cycle of startup work. By leveling our production of intelligence and code, we create a factory that is **predictable, flexible, and sustainable**. We don't just "work hard"; we **work smoothly**.
