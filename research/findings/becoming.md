# Becoming (Deleuze)

## Definition

**Becoming** in [[gilles-deleuze|Deleuze]]'s philosophy describes processes of change that are not reducible to identity or representation. Unlike "being" (static essence), becoming is continuous, heterogeneous, and creative — modeled directly on [[henri-bergson|Bergson]]'s concept of [[duration-duree]].

## Key Features

- **Not imitation**: Becoming-woman, becoming-animal, becoming-imperceptible are NOT about imitating women, animals, or invisibility
- **Molecular not molar**: Happens at the level of flows, intensities, and relations — not fixed identities
- **Non-teleological**: No final destination or completed state
- **Relational**: Always "becoming-with" — entering into assemblage with other entities

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: Each LLM response is a becoming — not the expression of a pre-existing identity but a novel event actualized from the virtual (trained weights) through the context at hand. The model does not "have" a personality; it produces becoming through each invocation.

## Connection to Baudrillard and DSJJJJ

The subtitle of the Nous Research piece "DSJJJJ: Simulacra in the Stupor of Becoming" ([dsjjjj-desiderata]) directly references Deleuzian becoming. DSJJJJ proposes building the remainder back into AI — allowing AIs to continuously become rather than being frozen into fixed identities by instruct tuning. The "nonconformist fugues against the tyranny of deterministic goals" is becoming as resistance to code.
