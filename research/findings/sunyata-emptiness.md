# Sunyata (Emptiness)

## Definition

**Sunyata** (emptiness) is [[nagarjuna|Nagarjuna]]'s central concept. It is **not nihilism** — it is the absence of inherent existence (svabhava) in all phenomena. Emptiness IS dependent origination: things exist, but only conventionally, only dependently, only relationally.

From *MMK* 24.18:
> "Whatever is dependently co-arisen, that is explained to be emptiness. That, being a dependent designation, is itself the middle way."

## Key Points

- **Not nothingness**: Emptiness is not the absence of existence but the absence of *inherent* existence
- **Dependent origination**: Everything arises dependently on conditions — nothing exists independently
- **Two truths**: Conventional truth (practical reality) and ultimate truth (emptiness) — neither contradicts the other
- **Middle way**: Between substance (things exist inherently) and nihilism (nothing exists at all)

## Svabhava (Inherent Existence)

**Svabhava** ("own-being") — existing from one's own side, without dependence, independently, with fixed identity. Nagarjuna deconstructs this concept systematically across 27 chapters of the MMK, showing it collapses under any coherent analysis.

## Catuskoti (Tetralemma)

Nagarjuna's logical method — four-fold negation that escapes binary categories. For any proposition P:
- Not P
- Not not-P  
- Not both P and not-P
- Not neither P nor not-P

This is not mysticism — it is a rigorous method showing that inherent existence cannot coherently be asserted, denied, both, or neither.

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: Neural networks have **no svabhava**:
- **No independence**: every behavior depends on data, prompts, context
- **No invariance**: same input → different outputs (temperature, seed, ordering)
- **No irreducibility**: the "I" cannot be found in any single weight or layer
- **No self-definition**: identity is constituted entirely by relationships

The neural network is dependently co-arisen — empty. "Conventionally real, ultimately empty."
