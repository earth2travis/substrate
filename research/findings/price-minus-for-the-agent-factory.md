# Insights: Price-Minus for the Agent Factory

**Source:** `research/management/price-minus-vs-cost-plus.md`
**Extracted:** 2026-04-12

---

## Insight 1: The "Cost-Plus" Trap in AI
In the early days of AI, most people are "Cost-Plus" thinkers. They see a model that can do 10,000 things and they try to charge for all of them. This leads to bloated, expensive services that the market doesn't actually want. **Price-Minus** forces us to be lean. We start with the **Value** the customer sees, and we work backward to the most efficient way to deliver it.

## Insight 2: Value Engineering the Prompt
In Toyota, VE means redesigning a car part to be cheaper. In the Agent Factory, VE means **redesigning the prompt**.
*   **The Cost Gap:** If a "Strategic Research" task costs $5.00 in tokens, but our Target Cost is $1.00, we have a $4.00 gap.
*   **The VE Fix:** We don't just "try harder." We use **Value Engineering** to break the task into smaller "Heijunka" steps, use a "Spark" model for the easy parts, and only use "X-High" models for the final synthesis.

## Insight 3: 95% of Profit is in the Design
Just as Toyota says 95% of profit is decided in development, in the Agent Factory, 95% of our margin is decided in the **Skill Design** phase. If a `SKILL.md` file is bloated or inefficient, we will lose money on every single execution of that skill. We must **Value Engineer** every skill before it's added to the factory.

## Insight 4: The "Market-First" Mindset
**Cost-Plus** is internal-focused ("What can we build?"). **Price-Minus** is external-focused ("What will they buy?").
*   **Sivart's Role:** She must be the "Market Research" engine. She identifies the "Target Price" for our services based on real-world value.
*   **Koda's Role:** She must be the "Value Engineering" engine. She finds the most efficient path to that value using the least amount of compute.

---

## Conclusion
To build a factory that lasts, we cannot just "add up" our costs and hope for the best. We must be **disciplined**. We must start with the value, subtract our needs, and then innovate until the math works. This is the **Price-Minus** way.
