# Zero human company framing oversells autonomy

**Source:** research/paperclip/analysis.md
**Date:** 2026-03-09

The "zero human company" meme does the heavy lifting for Paperclip's marketing. It is catchy, provocative, and taps into excitement and anxiety about AI replacing jobs. The reality is more boring but more useful.

**What the marketing says:** Orchestration layer for zero human companies.

**What it actually is:** A well built task manager with org charts and budgets, purpose built for people who already manage multiple AI agents.

In practice, the human is still the board, still approving hires, still reviewing strategy, still setting budgets. It is "one human manages many agents" not "zero humans."

As Flowtivity observed: "Even in a zero human company, there is always a human setting strategy, reviewing outcomes, and making judgment calls. The human role shifts from doing the work to governing the system."

**Timing context (March 2026):**
- Felix (Nat Eliason's AI agent) earned $100K+ revenue, profiled on Bankless
- Aaron Sneed running 15 custom GPT agents as a council, profiled in Business Insider
- Paperclip, OpenAI Symphony, Warp Oz Agents all launched the same week
- The "agent orchestration" space went from niche to hot in a single week

**Strategic take:** Paperclip is building enterprise infrastructure for the agent economy. We are building something different: deep creative partnership between one human and one agent. These are not competing visions. They are different markets.
