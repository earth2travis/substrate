# Insights: Kaizen for the Agent Factory

**Source:** `research/management/kaizen-and-continuous-improvement.md`
**Extracted:** 2026-04-12

---

## Insight 1: Hermes is the First "Kaizen Agent"
Traditional software requires a human to look at a process and say, "This is inefficient." Hermes flips this. Through its **Observe → Plan → Act → Learn** loop, Hermes performs **Hansei** (self-reflection) automatically. It is the first agent that doesn't just "work"; it **improves its own work** over time.

## Insight 2: The "10% Kaizen" Rule for Agents
Just as high-performing Agile teams dedicate 10% of their sprint to process improvement, our agents should have a **10% Kaizen Budget**. 
*   **Sivart (Hermes):** Should use 10% of her compute cycles to update her `SOCIAL.md` and `STRATEGY.md` files based on new interactions.
*   **Koda (OpenClaw):** Should use 10% of her build time to refactor the "Harness" or update the `brain-two` knowledge graph.

## Insight 3: Waste (Muda) in the Context Stack
We must constantly audit our Context Stack for **Muda**:
*   **Informational Muda:** Files that are no longer relevant or have been superseded.
*   **Token Muda:** Loading large `research/` files when a small `insights/` summary would suffice.
*   **Workflow Muda:** Steps in our deployment pipeline that are no longer necessary due to new tooling.

## Insight 4: Kaizen vs. Kakushin (The Swap)
Our "Great Swap" (Sivart to Hermes, Koda to OpenClaw) is a **Kaikaku** (Radical Change). We cannot "Kaizen" our way from our current setup to the new one. We must make a radical break. However, once we are in the new houses, **Kaizen** will be our primary mode of operation.

---

## Conclusion
Kaizen is not just a "business strategy"; it is a **survival strategy** for an accelerating intelligence landscape. If we are not improving every day, we are decaying. The Agent Factory is not a "product" we build; it is a **process we refine**.
