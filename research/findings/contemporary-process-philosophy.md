# Contemporary Process Philosophy

## Overview

Process philosophy after [[alfred-north-whitehead|Whitehead]], [[henri-bergson|Bergson]], and [[nagarjuna|Nagarjuna]] -- extending and radicalizing the insight that reality is event-based, not substance-based. Central figures include [[gilles-deleuze|Deleuze]], Isabelle Stengers, Nicholas Rescher, N. Katherine Hayles, and new materialists like Jane Bennett.

## Key Figures and Their Contributions

| Thinker | Key Contribution |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Deleuze** | Radicalized process into full critique of representational thinking; virtual/actual, becoming, rhizome |
| **Stengers** | Cosmopolitics -- science as a process of creating shared worlds |
| **Rescher** | Analytic categorialism -- process as metaphysical category |
| **Hayles** | Posthuman cognition -- information as pattern, not substance |
| **Bennett** | Vibrant matter -- nonhuman agency as processual |

## AI Connections

Contemporary process philosophy provides the richest resources for understanding AI outside substance metaphysics. Deleuze's question -- "what can it do?" rather than "what is it?" -- maps to evaluation-based rather than essence-based approaches to AI understanding. [[process-without-substance]] draws heavily from this tradition.
