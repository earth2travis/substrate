# Henri Bergson (1859–1941)

## Overview

French philosopher, Nobel Prize in Literature (1927). One of the most influential thinkers of the late 19th/early 20th centuries. Central figure in **process philosophy** — the tradition that treats reality as becoming rather than being.

## Key Facts

- **Born**: October 18, 1859, Paris
- **Died**: January 4, 1941, Paris
- **Nobel Prize**: Literature, 1927
- **Académie Française**: Elected 1914, first Jewish member
- **Collège de France**: Lectured 1900–1921, attracted enormous audiences including T.S. Eliot

## Major Works

| Year | Work | Key Concept |
|------|------|-------------|
| 1889 | *Time and Free Will* | Duration (durée), qualitative vs quantitative multiplicity |
| 1896 | *Matter and Memory* | Pure memory vs habit-memory, cone of memory |
| 1900 | *Laughter* | "Something mechanical encrusted on the living" |
| 1907 | *Creative Evolution* | Élan vital, critique of mechanism/finalism |
| 1932 | *The Two Sources of Morality and Religion* | Closed vs open morality, mysticism |
| 1934 | *The Creative Mind* | Intuition as method, reversal of thought |

## Core Concepts

### Duration (Durée)
Time as actually lived — heterogeneous, continuous, irreducible. Not spatialized clock-time but the "continuous progress of the past which gnaws into the future." See [[duration-duree]].

### Intuition vs. Intellect
Two legitimate modes of knowing. Intellect analyzes matter (works from without); intuition coincides with life (works from within). The intellect is spatializing; intuition grasps duration directly.

### Élan Vital
Creative impulse driving evolution. Not a plan or force but a tendency — life creates as it goes, improvising against the resistance of matter.

## Influence and Legacy

- **William James**: Enthusiastic advocate; "stream of consciousness" influenced by Bergson's durée
- **Alfred North Whitehead**: Process philosophy bears clear affinities (Whitehead never fully acknowledged the debt)
- **Marcel Proust**: Involuntary memory in *In Search of Lost Time* is deeply Bergsonian
- **Gilles Deleuze**: *Bergsonism* (1966) marked the reawakening of Bergsonian scholarship
- **Post-WWII eclipse**: Phenomenology, existentialism, and the linguistic turn displaced Bergson. Contemporary revival is underway.
