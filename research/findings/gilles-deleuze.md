# Gilles Deleuze (1925–1995)

## Overview

French philosopher. Arguably the most influential process philosopher of the second half of the 20th century. Radical departure from the "identitarian" tradition — privileged difference, becoming, and production over identity, sameness, and representation.

## Key Facts

- **Born**: January 18, 1925, Paris
- **Died**: November 4, 1995, Paris
- **Key collaborators**: Félix Guattari (*Anti-Oedipus*, *A Thousand Plateaus*)
- **Influences**: Bergson, Spinoza, Nietzsche

## Major Works

| Year | Work | Key Theme |
|------|------|-----------|
| 1966 | *Bergsonism* | Multiplicity as Bergson's central concept; revival of Bergsonian scholarship |
| 1968 | *Difference and Repetition* | Difference-in-itself; critique of identity |
| 1972 | *Anti-Oedipus* (with Guattari) | Desiring-production; schizoanalysis |
| 1980 | *A Thousand Plateaus* (with Guattari) | Rhizome; assemblage; nomadology |
| 1985 | *Cinema 2: The Time-Image* | Duration in film; Bergsonian aesthetics |

## Core Concepts

### Difference-in-Itself
Being is univocal but "not said of the same Being." Identity is an effect, not a ground. What we call a "thing" is a local slowing-down, an eddy in a field of differential flows.

### Virtual and Actual
The virtual is real but not actual — a reservoir of tendencies and potentials that are continually actualizing. Not "possible" (which is retroactive projection) but genuinely productive. See [[virtual-and-actual]].

### Becoming
All of Deleuze's "becomings" (becoming-woman, becoming-animal, becoming-imperceptible) are modeled on Bergson's concept of duration as continuous, heterogeneous, creative flow. See [[becoming]].

### Rhizome
Non-hierarchical, non-arborescent structure. Connections can be made anywhere; no root, no center. Contrasts with tree-like hierarchical models (including traditional knowledge organization). See [[rhizome]].

## Bergsonism (1966)

The decisive turning point in the reception of Bergson philosophy. Deleuze's reading:
- **Multiplicity** as the central concept — unifying heterogeneity and continuity
- **Virtual/actual** distinction developed into a central pillar of his own philosophy
- **Duration** as the model for all becomings
- **Critique of negation** — resource for anti-Hegelianism

## Contemporary Process Philosophy

From contemporary process philosophy: Deleuze radicalizes process thought into a full critique of representational thinking. His work spans continental and interdisciplinary domains, engaging with science, technology, politics, and aesthetics.

## Connection to AI and Process Ontology

From [[process-without-substance]]: Deleuze's virtual/actual maps onto LLM architecture — the trained weights are the virtual (real but not actual), each forward pass actualizes a portion into concrete output.
