# Memex

Vannevar Bush's 1945 concept of a mechanized personal library -- a desk-like device that stores all of a person's books, records, and communications, with the ability to link related items through associative trails.

## Historical Significance

Bush's "As We May Think" (The Atlantic, July 1945) imagined a device that works the way the human mind works: by association rather than alphabetical or numerical indexing. This was decades before the World Wide Web.

## Influence

The memex directly inspired:
- Ted Nelson's hypertext and Xanadu system
- Douglas Engelbart's oN-Line System (NLS) and the computer mouse
- The World Wide Web's linking model
- Personal wikis, Zettelkasten systems
- The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] -- the modern LLM-maintained persistent knowledge base is the memex realized with AI as the associate
