# Taiichi Ohno

> Japanese industrial engineer and businessman, widely recognized as the father of the Toyota Production System and one of the architects of lean manufacturing.

## Key Facts

- **Born**: February 29, 1912
- **Died**: May 28, 1990
- **Role**: Toyota executive who developed the Toyota Production System
- **Known as**: Father of TPS, Father of Lean Manufacturing

## Contributions

Developed the Toyota Production System after WWII:
- Just-in-Time (JIT) production
- Jidoka (Autonomation)
- Elimination of seven wastes (muda)
- Kanban system for pull production
- Andon cord for immediate problem-stop authority

## Philosophy

- Studied Ford's mass production and adapted it for Japan's resource constraints
- Believed waste elimination was the path to efficiency
- Emphasized respect for people as equal to technical innovation
- Continuous improvement (Kaizen) as a daily practice, not a one-time event

## Legacy

- Most influential manufacturing engineer of the 20th century
- TPS became the global model for [[lean-production]]
- Wrote "Toyota Production System: Beyond Large-Scale Production" (1988)
- His methods are now standard across automotive, electronics, healthcare, and software
