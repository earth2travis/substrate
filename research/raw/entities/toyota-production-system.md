# Toyota Production System (TPS)

> Manufacturing system developed by Toyota that became the foundation of lean production worldwide. Built on two pillars: Just-in-Time and Jidoka.

## Origin

- Developed in post-WWII Japan by **Taiichi Ohno** and others at Toyota
- Resource scarcity and limited capital forced innovation
- Studied Ford's mass production methods and adapted them for Japan's context

## Two Pillars

### Just-in-Time (JIT)
- Make only what is needed, when needed, in the amount needed
- Pull production based on customer orders
- Kanban cards signal when to produce
- Takt time sets production rhythm to customer demand

### Jidoka (Autonomation)
- Build quality into the process itself
- Machines stop automatically when problems occur
- Workers can stop the production line (Andon cord)
- Problem-solving happens immediately at the source

## Key Principles

- Continuous improvement (Kaizen)
- Respect for people
- Elimination of seven wastes (muda)
- Visual management (Andon, Kanban boards)
- Gemba - go to the actual place

## Global Impact

- Became the model for [[lean-production]] worldwide
- MIT IMVP Study documented Toyota's superiority over mass production
- "The Machine That Changed the World" (1990) popularized the term "Lean"
- Adopted by companies far beyond automotive
