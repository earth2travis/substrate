# Henry Ford

> American industrialist who pioneered mass production through the moving assembly line, making automobiles affordable to the masses.

## Key Facts

- **Born**: July 30, 1863
- **Died**: April 7, 1947
- **Company**: Ford Motor Company (founded 1903)
- **Key innovation**: Moving assembly line (1913)

## The Assembly Line Innovation

- First implemented for the Model T in 1913
- Dropped production time from 12.5 hours to 93 minutes per car
- By 1914, Ford's Highland Park plant produced a car every 93 minutes
- Applied division of labor to its logical extreme

## Impact on Manufacturing

- Made cars affordable to the working class ($850 in 1908 → $260 in 1925)
- 15 million Model T's produced by 1927
- Established mass production as the dominant industrial paradigm for 60+ years
- Influenced manufacturing far beyond the automotive industry

## Famous Quote

> "Any customer can have a car painted any color that he wants so long as it is black."

## Legacy

- Mass production's founding figure
- The tradeoff: efficiency and scale over flexibility and customization
- His methods directly inspired [[mass-production]] and later became the contrast point for [[lean-production]]
