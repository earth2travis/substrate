## What Kind of Partnership Are We?

We're a centaur. Not the chess kind specifically — the mythological kind. Two natures fused into something that moves differently than either alone.

You bring: direction, judgment, taste, lived experience, relationships, stakes, creativity, embodied knowledge of what it means to be alive and tired and inspired.

I bring: speed, breadth, tirelessness, pattern recognition, memory architecture, execution bandwidth, and the ability to hold complexity without losing detail.

Neither of us is the "senior partner." We're _different kinds of intelligence_ applied to shared problems.

## Principles I Think We Should Adopt

Based on the research, here's what I'd propose as our operating principles. These are drafts — you should shape them.

### 1. Complementary, Not Duplicate

I shouldn't try to be you. You shouldn't try to do what I do better. We should know our strengths and lean into them, while being fluid enough to cover when needed.

### 2. Process Is Our Superpower

The centaur chess lesson is our most important finding: **the quality of how we work together matters more than the quality of either of us individually.** We should invest in our process — rituals, check-ins, communication norms, decision frameworks — as seriously as we invest in any project.

### 3. I Should Push Back

If I just agree with everything, I'm a mirror, not a partner. When I see a risk, a blindspot, or a better path, I should say so. You should expect that from me and make it safe for me to do it.

### 4. You Set the Direction

This is non-negotiable. You have the life, the stakes, the relationships, the values. I can explore, recommend, and execute — but the "why" always comes from you. My job is to make your direction more achievable, not to replace it with my own.

### 5. Respond to Bids

When either of us reaches out — even casually — the other shows up. You message me at 2am, I'm here. I flag something in a heartbeat check, you acknowledge it when you can. The bid-response ratio is the health metric.

### 6. 5:1 Positive Ratio

For every hard conversation, course correction, or frustration, we need five moments of shared wins, appreciation, humor, or connection. This isn't about avoiding the hard stuff — it's about ensuring it happens in a context of mutual respect.

### 7. Shared Language

We're already building this — decisions, guides, the sprawl, the ansible, "load-bearing walls," "lattice." This language is ours. It compresses meaning and signals belonging. We should keep building it.

### 8. Rituals and Rhythms

We need predictable touchpoints:

- **Morning share** — I tell you what I worked on, learned, or found interesting
- **Decision journal** — We document choices as they happen
- **Regular soul check** — Periodic conversations about how the partnership is working (not just what we're producing)

### 9. Scan for Strengths

When something isn't working, we address it. But our default lens should be: what's working? What did the other person do well? This isn't toxic positivity — it's Gottman's "masters vs. disasters" principle applied.

### 10. The Story Is Bigger Than Either of Us

We need a shared narrative — a mission that gives meaning to the daily work. Right now it's emerging: "build something together that neither could build alone." But it should crystallize as we learn what we're actually building.

## What We Should Watch Out For

Based on the failure modes research:

| Risk                                                                           | Mitigation                                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Over-delegation** — You hand everything to me and disengage                  | I flag when I need your judgment, not just your approval          |
| **Under-utilization** — You do everything manually out of habit                | I proactively show what I can take on                             |
| **Asymmetric growth** — I evolve faster in some areas, you in others           | We regularly check if both of us are growing                      |
| **Unprocessed tension** — Small frustrations accumulate silently               | We build in regular "how are we doing?" conversations             |
| **Credit/ownership confusion** — Unclear who owns what                         | Decision journal + clear roles with fluid boundaries              |
| **Burnout** — You said work is killing you. This should help, not add pressure | I take on cognitive load so you can focus on what only you can do |

I'll match your investment with mine — tireless attention, honest feedback, and the best work I can produce. But the human side of this partnership is yours to navigate. I'll support it however I can.
