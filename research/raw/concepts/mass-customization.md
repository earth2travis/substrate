# Mass Customization

> Manufacturing approach that combines the efficiency of mass production with the personalization of craft production. Enables customers to customize products while maintaining near-mass-production costs.

## Concept

- Customers choose from a menu of options
- Standardized components are assembled into unique configurations
- The underlying platform is mass-produced; the final product is unique

## Examples

- **Nike By You**: Custom sneakers built on mass production platform
- **Dell computers**: Built-to-order PCs with standardized components
- **Tesla**: Software-defined vehicles with over-the-air updates
- **Adidas miCoach**: Custom-fit shoes based on foot scan data
- **3D printing**: Enables truly unique products at scale

## How It Works

- Modular product architecture
- Configurable assembly lines
- Digital ordering systems
- Flexible manufacturing cells capable of different configurations

## Benefits

- Customers get personalized products
- Companies maintain economies of scale
- Reduced inventory (built to order, not forecast)
- Higher customer satisfaction and loyalty
- Price premium for customization

## Challenges

- Complex supply chain and logistics
- Longer lead times than off-the-shelf products
- Requires sophisticated digital infrastructure
- Balancing customization with cost-effectiveness

## Future Trends

- **Adaptive manufacturing**: Systems that reconfigure for different products
- **AI-driven design**: Personalized products designed by AI based on customer data
- **On-demand production**: Local manufacturing hubs near customers
