# Obeya (Big Room)

## Definition

**Obeya** (大部屋, literally "big room") is a visual management system used at Toyota where all key stakeholders gather in a shared space with visual displays of project status, challenges, and milestones. It is the physical manifestation of the [[chief-engineer-system|Shusa]]'s integration role.

## How It Works

- **Visual displays**: Charts, drawings, timelines, and metrics cover the walls
- **Cross-functional**: Representatives from all departments attend regular meetings
- **Decision-oriented**: The goal is rapid decision-making, not information reporting
- **Shusa-led**: The Shusa (Chief Engineer) runs the meetings and makes trade-off decisions on the spot
- **Transparent**: All information is visible to everyone — no hidden status or siloed data

## Connection to Lean

Obeya supports [[just-in-time]] by making the current state of development visible to everyone simultaneously, reducing delays from information asymmetry. It enables [[kaizen]] by making problems visible and actionable.

## Modern Equivalent

In software development, the Obeya maps to war rooms, incident bridges, and increasingly to digital dashboards. For Zookooree, the Obeya could be a shared observability dashboard showing all agent product states in real-time.
