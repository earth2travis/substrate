# Kaizen

> Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement through small, incremental changes made by all employees. A core principle of [[lean-production]].

## Meaning

- **Kai** (改) = change
- **Zen** (善) = good
- Together: "change for the better" or "continuous improvement"

## Key Principles

- Incremental improvements, not revolutionary changes
- All employees participate -- from CEO to frontline workers
- Daily practice, not periodic events
- Focus on processes, not blame
- Suggestion systems to capture employee ideas
- Standardization followed by improvement

## Benefits

- Sustains improvements over decades
- Employees feel ownership of processes
- Compounds to significant gains over time
- Low-cost implementation

## Cultural Challenges

- Western cultures often favor dramatic changes over incremental ones
- Requires management commitment to employee suggestions
- Success depends on trust between workers and management
- Difficult to measure ROI on individual suggestions
