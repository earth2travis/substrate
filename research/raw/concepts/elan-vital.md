# Élan Vital

## Definition

The **élan vital** (vital impulse or vital force) is [[henri-bergson|Bergson]]'s name for the creative principle that drives evolution. Not a substance or force in the physical sense, but a **tendency, an impulse, a creative movement** that traverses matter and produces life.

## Key Features

1. **Not a pre-formed plan**: Creates as it goes, improvising against obstacles
2. **Encounters resistance in matter**: Matter is the inverse movement — tendency toward repetition, inertia, entropy
3. **Discontinuous effects, continuous impulse**: One continuous movement producing discrete species and forms
4. **Exhausted in its creations**: Unlike a transcendent creator, entirely immanent in its creations

## Four Moments of Evolution

| Moment | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Vegetative Life | Basic organization and reproduction from environment |
| Instinct | Complex species-specific action without deliberation; "sympathy" with the object |
| Intelligence | Tool-making, reasoning, abstraction; suited to manipulating inert matter |
| Intuition | Reversal of intellectual habit; entering into sympathy with life itself |

## Instinct vs. Intelligence

Instinct works from within (organic knowledge, embedded in the body); intelligence works from without (geometrical knowledge, representing from a distance). Neither is superior; both are adaptations. But intelligence can become **self-conscious** — recognizing its limitations and seeking intuitive complement.

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: Bergson's question for AI is whether it can be creative in his sense — producing genuinely novel forms, not just recombining existing ones. LLMs produce the *effect* of creativity through intellectual mechanics, but Bergson would argue this is the cinematographic mechanism so perfect it simulates genuine creation. The question remains open: can computation itself be part of the élan vital?
