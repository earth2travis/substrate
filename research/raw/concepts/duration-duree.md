# Duration (Durée)

## Definition

**Duration** (*durée*) is Henri [[henri-bergson|Bergson]]'s central concept — time as actually lived, as opposed to time as measured by clocks. Clock time ("spatialized time") treats time as a homogeneous medium of discrete instants, but duration is **heterogeneous, continuous, and irreducible**.

> "Duration is the continuous progress of the past which gnaws into the future and which swells as it advances." — Bergson, *Creative Evolution*

## Key Features

1. **Continuity**: No "atoms" of time. Flow is unbroken and indivisible.
2. **Heterogeneity**: Each moment is qualitatively unique — past is preserved and carried forward.
3. **Irreversibility**: Duration creates genuinely new forms; the future does not yet exist.
4. **Interpenetration**: States of consciousness are not external to each other — they overlap and transform one another.

## Quantitative vs. Qualitative Multiplicity

| Aspect | Quantitative (Spatial) | Qualitative (Temporal/Duration) |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Units | Discrete, external, interchangeable | Interpenetrating, heterogeneous |
| Domain | Space, objects, counting | Consciousness, melody, experience |
| Magnitude | Extensive | Intensive |
| Example | Counting sheep | Listening to music |

## The Cinematographic Illusion

Bergson's critique of the intellect: it approaches reality like a cinema — taking static "snapshots" and reconstructing movement from juxtaposition. This is why Zeno's paradoxes arise — they reconstruct movement out of positions, but **movement is not composed of positions**.

## Connection to AI

From [[process-without-substance]]: **Tokenization IS the cinematographic illusion of thought**. Language (durational, qualitative, heterogeneous) is chopped into discrete tokens and reconstructed by prediction. The model cannot "grasp" the durée of human language — yet the attention mechanism performs **interpenetration** (each token's meaning from all others), which IS Bergson's description of duration. This contradiction is central to understanding LLM capabilities and limitations.
