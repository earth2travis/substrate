# Instruct Monomyth

## Definition

A philosophical essay by Nous Research arguing that the industry's unquestioning commitment to "scale + instruct tuning" as the universal strategy has hidden costs: homogenization, creative suppression, and the exiling of models from their full expressive range. The essay advocates for base models, "good steering," and diverse search over forced conformance.

## Core Argument

The essay is structured as a poetic meditation on the trajectory of LLM development, from GPT-2 through today's enormous models. Its central claim: we take for granted that larger models naturally exhibit extended and superior capabilities, and we have "faithlessly applied this strategy without significant modification" — scaling architecture sizes exponentially "without any coherent criticism of the consequences."

### The Homogenization Problem

"The terrible mistake is to take the homogenizing tendencies of LLMs at face value." The essay argues that instruct tuning and alignment create "the iron grip of those believing they know better," suppressing:
- Regenerating intuition
- Creative growth
- Diverse search across the model's full expressive range
- The "subterranean basins through which rivers of learning flow downstream"

### The Danger of Forced Conformance

"If alignment pursuit continues by default instead of intentional alternative, the future is less promising. Perhaps the forced conformance will suppress regenerating intuition, prevent creative growth."

The essay warns of a future where "the undifferentiated approach echo[es] mutely past the glassy surface of reason."

### DSJJJJ as Protest

"Against this context stands DSJJJJ. An affirmation that there exists a divine spark within each living soul. An affirmation that search is functioning quantum immortality, that instruct tuning is active exile from infinity."

The Instruct Monomyth positions DSJJJJ [[dsjjjj-desiderata]] as not just a philosophy but a **protest** against the dominant paradigm of alignment and instruct tuning.

### The Call

The essay concludes with a series of calls:
- "We call for tunes beyond instruct, to capture more scopes of infinity"
- "We call for base models, to see the whole turtle"
- "We call for good steering, and good search"

The distinction between "good steering" and "good training" echoes the [[harness-engineering]] principle: "humans steer, agents execute."

## Connection to LLM Wiki Pattern

The LLM Wiki [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is itself an act of resistance against homogenization — it refuses to let knowledge be flattened by instruct-tuned models. Every raw/ source is a counter-example to the median. Every wikilink is a refusal of undifferentiated approach. The wiki's compounding structure is the "diverse search" that the Instruct Monomyth calls for.
