# Just-in-Time (JIT)

> Production and inventory management system where goods are produced and delivered only when needed, in the exact amount needed. One of the two pillars of the Toyota Production System.

## Core Principle

Make only what is needed, when needed, in the amount needed. This contrasts sharply with mass production's "push" approach of building large batches in advance.

## How It Works

- **Pull system**: Production triggered by actual customer demand
- **Kanban cards**: Visual signals that tell upstream processes when to produce more
- **Takt time**: Production rhythm set to match customer demand rate
- **Small batches**: Reduces inventory and increases flexibility

## Benefits

- Dramatically lower inventory costs
- Less capital tied up in stock
- Faster detection of quality problems
- More responsive to demand changes

## Vulnerabilities

- Requires reliable, nearby suppliers
- Fragile to disruptions (natural disasters, pandemics, geopolitical events)
- Post-pandemic: companies adding strategic safety stocks alongside JIT
