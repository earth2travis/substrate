# Price-Minus vs. Cost-Plus Pricing

**Researched:** 2026-04-12
**Topic:** Target Costing, Value Engineering, Lean Accounting

---

## I. Overview
Most businesses use **Cost-Plus Pricing**: they calculate how much it costs to build a product, add a profit margin, and set the price. Toyota, however, uses **Price-Minus Pricing** (also known as Target Costing). They start with the price the customer is willing to pay, subtract the profit they need to survive, and the remaining amount is the **Target Cost**.

**The Formula:**
*   **Cost-Plus:** Cost + Profit = Price
*   **Price-Minus:** Price - Profit = Target Cost

## II. The Toyota Approach
At Toyota, the "Target Cost" is not a goal; it is a **constraint**.
1.  **Market Research:** Determine what the customer will pay for a specific set of features.
2.  **Profit Goal:** Determine the profit needed to sustain the business.
3.  **The Gap:** If the engineering team estimates the car will cost $28,500 to build, but the Target Cost is $27,200, they have a $1,300 "cost gap."
4.  **Value Engineering (VE):** The team must redesign the product, source different parts, or streamline the process to close that $1,300 gap **before** production begins.

## III. Value Engineering vs. Value Analysis
*   **Value Engineering (VE):** Happens during **product development**. It's about designing the cost out of the product before it exists.
*   **Value Analysis (VA):** Happens during **production**. It's about finding small ways to reduce cost after the design is frozen.
*   **The 95% Rule:** Toyota believes that 95% of a product's profit is determined during the development phase. Once the first car rolls off the line, it's almost too late to change the profit margin.

## IV. Relevance to the Agent Factory
We are currently "Cost-Plus" thinkers. We look at what an agent *can* do, and we charge for it. To build a sustainable **Zookooree**, we must be "Price-Minus" thinkers.
*   **The Market Price:** What is a client willing to pay for a "fully audited code review"?
*   **The Profit Goal:** What do we need to keep the lights on and reinvest in R&D?
*   **The Target Cost:** How many tokens and how much compute time can we spend on that review while still hitting our profit goal?

If our "Cost-Plus" approach leads to a $500 cost for a $200 service, we don't raise the price. We use **Value Engineering** to redesign the agent's workflow until it costs $50.
